ABSTRACT: In this work there is a study on the skills of students from 10th and 11th grades of pre-
university for the simultaneous work with essential aspects of Data Treatment, Logics and Ele-
mentary Statistics, in the interpretation of the information obtained in a survey and presented in
a table of binary data, taking as reference the works of Pitarch (2002) and Orœs (1992), carried out
with students from the ESO in Spain and primary school in France respectively. By applying the
techniques of Implicative Statistical Analysis, this article shows the relationship between the logical
and statistical work through the treatment of data, detecting signi(cid:222) cant differences in the acquired
skills for students of two pre-universitary schools taken as sample for the experimentation. Finally
it is presented, with help of the ISA, a set of rules that show the relations between the logical and
statistical operations and the treatment of data.
